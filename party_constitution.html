Constitution of the Democratic Party of Ukraine

Article I - Name

Section 1. The name of this organization shall be the Democratic Unity Party of Ukraine abbreviated DPU hereafter referred to as "the Party".

Article II - Membership

Section 1. An application for membership may be received by the National Committee.

Section 2. All applications shall be accepted unless the National Committee has information that the application was false or that admitting the applicant would be harmful to the Party organization and its goals.

Section 3. Membership in the Party is completely voluntary.

Section 4. Membership may be terminated by the Party Congress.

Article III - National Congress

Section 1. The highest authority of the Party is the National Congress. It is authorized to make political and organizational decisions binding upon the entire Party. The decisions of the National Congress shall guide all Party organizations and members.

Section 2. Regular National Congress shall be held every four years.

Section 3. The National Congress shall be composed of delegates elected by all members of the Party.

Section 4. A special National Congress shall be called when there is an affirmative vote of 40 percent of the members of the Central Committee or a majority vote of members of the Party.

Section 5. Each National Congress shall determine the number of delegates and the date of the next National Congress.

Article IV - National Committee

Section 1. Between National Congresses, the National Committee is the highest authority of the Party, representing the Party as a whole.

Section 2. Vacancies in the National Committee may be filled by majority vote of the National Committee.

Section 3. The National Committee shall elect such officers as it decides upon. The National Committee shall establish such other committees as it deems necessary to carry out its work. It may delegate its powers and responsibilities to those officers or committees as appropriate.

Section 4. All such officers and committees shall be responsible to the National Committee.

Section 5. The National Committee shall be elected by the National Congress.

Article V - Amendment

Section 1. This Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds vote of any regular or special National Congress.
